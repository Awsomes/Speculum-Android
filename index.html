First and foremost

I did not come up with this all by myself, the idea came to me when I saw a similar project by Hannah Mitt. After searching some more and finding the smart mirror project by Michael Teeuw I knew I wanted to make something like this. As a Android developer with some free time I decided to give it a shot. I had an old Nexus 7 from 2012 lying around doing nothing but collecting dust and wanting a new purpose in his life. Speculum, as of now, is designed for that particular device only. It might look good on your device and I might look bad. Supporting multiple screensizes is on the roadmap but as of now the easiest way is to just adjust the layouts to your specific device. More on building this application can be found in the GitHub Readme.

Voice Recognition

Speculum comes with a couple of voice commands baked in. When you turn on the voice recognition and fire up your mirror say 'Hello magic mirror' to wake it! When the mirror is awake you can use a couple of commands such as a command for updating the view and telling a joke. The command phrases are fully customizable and are there merely to demonstrate the opportunities. It is very easy to integrate your owm commands into the app. You just have to change a little bit of code and write the implementation for you new custom command. For example; 'Hello magic mirror, turn on the lights for me' paired with your Philips Hue set-up in your bedroom would be a very nice idea. You can turn your old device in a full fledged home automation tool if you have the time and the fantasies, be creative! For the time being, take a look at the provided demo of the voice recognition feature. All the nescessary information about implementing your own voice commands is described in the GitHub page of this project.

Showcase

I live in The Netherlands and two-way mirrors are nearly impossible to find and if you do, very expensive. However it is possible to create the mirror yourself using plexiglass/glass and privacy foil used on windows. This will give you the desired effect but is significantly less beautiful then a real two-way mirror so if you can get your hands on those I would recommend you go that route. I used 4mm thick polished plexiglass and mirror privacy foil. I applied the foil on the plexiglass, built a little frame for the tablet and hanging mechanism and spray-painted the back pitch black (except of a square the size of the device, of course). I made a frame from wood to put around the mirror so the thing looks the real deal. The whole thing cost me maybe 30 bucks. The UI/UX designer used aluminium for his frame, giving the thing a modern look. Both finished products can be seen here. If you decide to do this project send me the end result so it can be displayed here as well!
